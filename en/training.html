Positive training

Training & socialisation

Training isn't a constraint — it's the love language you and your dog learn together.

The core principle: positive reinforcement

Our Nordic breeds are intelligent, sometimes stubborn, always sensitive. Methods based on physical punishment or forced dominance produce anxious or aggressive dogs. The scientifically validated method — and the only one we recommend — is positive reinforcement: reward what you want to see repeated, ignore what you don't want to encourage.

The golden rule: Your dog does something you like? Reward immediately (within 2 seconds). Your dog does something you don't want? Calmly interrupt, redirect, and reward the correct alternative behaviour.

First commands (8–16 weeks)

The socialisation window is between 3 and 16 weeks of age. After that, learning remains possible but requires more effort. Make the most of the first weeks to teach:

  • Sit — the foundational command that structures your relationship. Show a treat above the head, say "Sit" the moment the bottom touches the floor, reward immediately.
  • Down — from "Sit", gently guide the treat toward the floor. 10 repetitions per session, 2–3 sessions per day.
  • Recall — the most important command of all. Start in a closed space, call the name + "Come", reward generously every time. Never recall to scold.
  • Drop it / Leave it — critical for Pomsky and Husky who pick up everything. Always offer a treat exchange — never snatch forcefully.

Breed-specific tips

Pomsky

The Pomsky combines Husky intelligence (fast boredom) with Pomeranian stubbornness. It learns quickly but may unilaterally decide the lesson is over. Key: keep sessions short (5–8 minutes maximum), varied, and always end on a success.

Pomeranian

Watch out for "small dog syndrome" — the tendency to let things slide that you'd never accept from a large breed. A Pomeranian that growls over its food bowl is a behavioural issue, regardless of size. Tiny doesn't mean excused.

Siberian Husky

The Husky is not a submissive dog. It evolved working as a team, taking initiative. It will respect you as a partner, not an absolute master. Intense physical exercise before training sessions dramatically improves concentration.

Toilet training

Puppies cannot control their bladder before 12–16 weeks. Until then: take them out immediately after every meal, every nap, every play session. Reward consistently outdoors. Accident indoors: clean with an enzyme cleaner (not ammonia), no punishment — they genuinely don't understand retrospective scolding.

Most puppies from Cimes Blanches are 90% toilet-trained within 3–4 weeks at home, provided the protocol is followed consistently.
