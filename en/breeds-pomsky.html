The Pomsky

Pomeranian × Siberian Husky — the unlikely match that conquered the world.

Origins

The story of the Pomsky

The Pomsky was born from a desire: to capture the fascinating eyes and splendid coat of the Siberian Husky in a size better suited to apartment living. The cross between the Pomeranian (Spitz dwarf) and the Husky gave birth to what is now one of the most photographed dogs in the world.

Appearing in the United States in the early 2010s, the Pomsky spread rapidly across Europe. Its success lies in a rare combination: beauty, intelligence, and a playful temperament compatible with family life.

Adult size: between 20 and 35cm at the withers depending on generation (F1, F2, F3). Weight: 5 to 14 kg.

An extraordinary personality

The Pomsky inherits the best of both worlds: the alertness and energy of the Husky, softened by the affection and liveliness of the Pomeranian. It is curious, intelligent, sometimes scatter-brained — a quick learner who will cheerfully test limits.

An excellent family dog, it gets along beautifully with children and enjoys the company of other animals. One caution: its Nordic instinct gives it a tendency to wander — the garden must be securely fenced.
