🐾 Adoption & reservation

How do I reserve a puppy? +
Reservation starts with direct contact (contact form or phone), followed by a visit to the kennel. A deposit of CHF 500 is required to confirm your reservation; the balance is settled on the day the puppy leaves. We do not accept any firm reservation without a prior visit — we want to see you interact with our dogs.
At what age do puppies leave? +
Our puppies leave the kennel at a minimum of 8 weeks, in accordance with Swiss animal welfare legislation. Some puppies stay until 10–12 weeks if we feel they benefit from additional socialisation time.
Can we visit the kennel? +
Absolutely, and we strongly encourage it. Visits are by appointment only, Monday to Saturday. Please do not drop by unannounced — our dogs need undisturbed rest periods for their wellbeing.
Is there a waiting list? +
Yes, for the most sought-after puppies (blue-eyed Pomsky, orange Pomeranians). Joining the waiting list is free — a deposit is only requested when we confirm a litter matching your criteria.

💰 Prices & payment

What are your prices? +
Pomsky F1/F2: CHF 2,500–3,200 depending on coat and eye colour. Pomeranian: CHF 2,000–2,800. Siberian Husky: CHF 1,800–2,500. Prices include FCI pedigree, full health record, first vaccination, deworming, microchip, and a welcome kit (scent blanket, toy, transition kibbles).
What payment methods do you accept? +
Bank transfer (Swiss IBAN), Twint, and cash. We do not accept card payments. The balance must be settled before or on the day of departure — no puppy leaves with an outstanding invoice.
Is the deposit refundable? +
The deposit is refundable only if we are unable to fulfil the agreed litter. If you withdraw, the deposit remains with the kennel but may be transferred to a future litter within 12 months.

🩺 Health & vaccinations

What health guarantees do you offer? +
Every puppy leaves with a veterinary health certificate dated within 72 hours. We provide a 2-year genetic guarantee against serious hereditary conditions certified by our testing. Our breeding animals are all tested for hip dysplasia, cardiac, and ocular conditions.
Is the puppy vaccinated? +
Yes. Each puppy receives its first vaccination before departure, plus full deworming. The official health record is handed over with the puppy. Your vet will schedule the booster at 12 weeks.
Is the puppy microchipped? +
Yes, microchipping is mandatory in Switzerland and is done before departure. Initial registration in the AMICUS national database is included in the price. You will need to update your contact details in the system after adoption.

🐕 The breeds

Is the Pomsky suitable for apartment living? +
Yes, the Pomsky adapts very well to apartment life provided it gets sufficient exercise (two daily walks of at least 30 minutes each) and mental stimulation. It can be vocal when under-stimulated — worth considering if you have close neighbours.
Pomsky F1, F2, F3 — what's the difference? +
F1 = 50% Pomeranian / 50% Husky (direct cross). F2 = two F1 Pomskies crossed. F3 = two F2 Pomskies. Generally, the higher the generation, the smaller and more consistent the size. Our F1s are the largest (8–14kg) with the greatest appearance variability — often the most beautiful surprises.
Is a Husky the right fit for me? +
The Husky is an extraordinary dog — but a demanding one. It needs intense daily exercise, space, and company (it suffers alone). If you want a calm, low-maintenance dog, the Husky is probably not your match. If you love running, hiking, skiing — you will find the perfect partner.

🚗 Transport & delivery

Do you deliver puppies to other countries? +
We can arrange a halfway handover for families near the Swiss border (eastern France, Haute-Savoie, Rhône-Alpes). Home delivery is not offered. For longer distances, we can refer you to approved specialist pet transporters.
Can we collect our puppy on a weekend? +
Yes, Saturday handovers are available. We prefer to avoid Friday evenings (risk of the puppy arriving when everyone is about to be out all day Monday).
