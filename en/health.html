Health & prevention

A healthy dog begins with regular vet visits and attentive daily observation.

Travel note: To enter France or any EU country, rabies vaccination is compulsory. Allow 21 days between primary rabies vaccination and crossing the border.

Deworming & parasite control

  • Deworming (internal parasites): Every 3 months until 6 months of age, then twice yearly for life. In the Valais mountains, more frequent treatment is recommended (dogs in contact with wildlife)
  • Ticks: Prevention from March to November in Switzerland. Spot-on or collar (Seresto). Manual check after every woodland walk
  • Fleas: Monthly spot-on or quarterly treatment depending on product. If infestation occurs, treat the environment too (home, car)
  • Mosquitoes / heartworm: Relevant if you travel with your dog to southern France, Spain, or Italy — ask your vet before departure

Breed-specific conditions

Pomsky

  • Patellar luxation (genetically monitored in our breeding dogs)
  • Hip dysplasia (rare in small individuals, more common in heavier F1s)
  • Hereditary eye conditions (annual testing on all our adults)
  • Alopecia X (hormonal coat loss — cosmetic, not painful)

Pomeranian

  • Patellar luxation (most common condition in small breeds)
  • Tracheal collapse (never use a collar — harness only, always)
  • Hypoglycaemia in very young puppies (watch closely from 8 to 14 weeks)
  • Dental problems (small jaw — annual scale and polish from 2 years)

Siberian Husky

  • Hereditary cataracts (annual CAER test on all our adults)
  • Progressive retinal atrophy (genetic test included)
  • Hip dysplasia (rare but possible in large-framed individuals)
  • Skin sensitivity (avoid harsh shampoos)

Warning signs — see a vet immediately

  • Repeated vomiting over 24 hours, or blood in vomit
  • Persistent bloody diarrhoea
  • Sudden marked lethargy, total food refusal lasting more than 48 hours
  • Distended, hard abdomen (absolute emergency: gastric torsion)
  • Chronic cough or laboured breathing
  • Lameness that does not resolve within 24 hours
  • Red eyes or heavy eye discharge
  • Seizures — immediate emergency vet visit
