New owner guide

Essential puppy care

The first weeks with your puppy are crucial. Here is everything you need to know — straight talk, no fluff.

Homecoming day

The day your puppy arrives is one of the most important moments of its life. It has just lost its mother, its littermates, and all its familiar scents. It's brave, but it's scared. Your job: stay calm, warm, and avoid over-exciting the puppy.

Key tip: Plan the arrival for a Friday evening or Saturday morning — you'll be present for the first two full days without having to rush off to work.
  • Prepare a defined space (puppy pen or corner of a room) so it can explore without getting lost in the whole house
  • Place the scent blanket from the kennel (included in the welcome kit) in its bed — scent is its first language
  • Avoid having the whole family visit on day one — let it adjust to you first
  • Don't force cuddles if it prefers to observe from a distance

The first days

The first 72 hours are often the most intense. The puppy may cry at night, refuse food, or alternatively eat too fast and regurgitate. All of this is normal. Stay calm. Don't bring it into your bed on the first night — unless you've decided that's where it will sleep forever.

Mistakes to avoid

  • Immediately changing its diet (we provide one week of transition kibble)
  • Leaving it alone for a full day before the end of the first week
  • Harshly scolding it for toileting indoors (it doesn't know yet)
  • Giving it toys that look like your shoes or cushions

Socialisation — the window that doesn't last

Between 3 and 12 weeks, a puppy's brain is in "maximum recording" mode. Everything it encounters in this period, it internalises as normal. Everything it doesn't encounter risks becoming a source of anxiety later.

Use this window to expose your puppy to:

  • Varied sounds: vacuum cleaner, lawnmower, rain, traffic, children
  • Different surfaces: grass, wood floors, tiles, gravel, snow
  • People of all types: children, elderly people, people wearing hats, glasses, beards
  • Other species: cats, rabbits (supervised)
Important: Socialisation before full vaccination should take place in controlled environments (friends' homes with vaccinated dogs, not public parks).
